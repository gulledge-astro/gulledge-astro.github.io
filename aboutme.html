About Me

“Though my soul may set in darkness, it will rise in perfect light” - Sarah Williams

>

My name is Deborah Gulledge, and I am a physicist with the 15th Space Surveillance Squadron in Maui, HI, where I research new techniques to collect and process data from ground-based electro-optical telescopes, and lead integration of hardware and software solutions to improve upon existing technology for Space Domain Awareness.

I earned my Ph.D. in Astronomy in November 2022 from Georgia State University, where I worked as an NSF Graduate Research Fellow with Stuart Jefferies. My research focused on Doppler imaging seismology of the Gas Giants to constrain both their internal structure and atmospheric dynamics, coupled with development of astronomical instrumentation to collect these measurements.

I previously also collaborated with Douglas Tucker, Sahar Allam, and others on absolute calibration for the Dark Energy Survey (DES) to better understand the nature of the dark energy that is accelerating the expansion of our Universe. This work enabled absolute calibration for the Dark Energy Survey (DES) to better understand the nature of dark energy.

I graduated with honors from Austin Peay State University in 2018 with a major in physics and a double minor in mathematics and astronomy. That same year I was named the Robert F. Sears Physics/Astronomy Award Recipient for an outstanding student in physics or astronomy, where the recipient must closely identify with the legacy of Dr. Sears and be enthusiastically committed to a lifelong dedication to the sciences. My research contributions were further recognized by the National Science Foundation through a NSF Graduate Research Fellowship.

At APSU I worked with Dr. J. Allyn Smith and collaborated Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory scientists Douglas Tucker, Sahar Allam, and others on absolute calibration for the Dark Energy Survey (DES) to better understand the nature of the dark energy that is accelerating the expansion of our Universe. This work enabled absolute calibration for the Dark Energy Survey (DES) to better understand the nature of dark energy.

My other research interests include astrobiology and general planetary science. Aside from research, I enjoy reading, sewing, scuba & freediving, hiking, and aerial silks. Please feel free to contact me with any questions!
