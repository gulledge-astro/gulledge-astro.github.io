About Me

I am a research physicist at the 15th Space Surveillance Squadron in Maui, HI, working in the Spectral Exploitation Technologies group, where I lead organic research in unresolved imaging and satellite characterization to support the improvement and application of directed energy and electro-optics technologies for Space Domain Awareness (SDA) at the Maui Space Surveillance Complex (MSSC). My graduate work focused on creating a novel instrument for Doppler imaging (the Planetary Multilevel Oscillations and Dynamics experiment, or PMODE) for the young field of planetary seismology to probe the internal structure and atmospheric dynamics of Jupiter. Broadly, I am interested in giant planet interiors and formation, Doppler imaging techniques, low- and high-resolution spectroscopy, time-domain analysis, and the development of new astronomical instrumentation. Outside of work, I enjoy hiking, scuba and freediving, sewing, and snuggling with my two cats.

Research

The interiors of our Gas Giant planets remain enshrouded, their detailed core structure remaining a mystery to today's astronomers. The structure of these interiors holds a key to explaining how the planets of the Solar System itself formed, whether from the bottom-up by core accretion, or from the top-down from gravitational instability. They are impossible to probe directly with current gravity field measurements of Jupiter; however, implementing seismic techniques may be able to probe through the thick veil of atmosphere and reveal the core structure directly. My work lies in developing novel astronomical instrumentation and observing on world-class telescopes in an attempt to finally answer this enduring question..
